# Engineering Entrepreneurship Programming Revamp

*Strategy and approval paper. The companion Product Studio document sets out the operating design for the Validate-stage program.*

## 1. The strategic case

MakerLaunch, the Faculty of Engineering's accelerator, is constrained not by its own design but by the absence of a pipeline feeding it. In the most recent cycle it received four applications, and only one met entry requirements. Advisor feedback consistently notes that applicant ventures are not at the stage the program expects.

This is not a failing accelerator. It is a missing validation stage between the Faculty's classroom programming and its accelerator. Engineering students generate ideas in GNG 4120, pitch competitions, and capstone projects, but no program carries those ideas forward into validated ventures. Students with entrepreneurial intent either stall at the course endpoint or leave the Faculty pipeline for outside programs. This revamp closes that gap.

## 2. Strategic intent

The intent is to build the validation pipeline that lets the accelerator work, and to develop more engineering students into venture-ready founders, through a connected four-stage pathway: Explore, Validate, Build, and Scale. CEED concentrates its resources on the first three stages and hands off to the broader ecosystem at Scale. MakerLaunch is repositioned as the Build-stage destination within a real pipeline, rather than a standalone program asked to produce ventures from unvalidated inputs.

| Stage | Purpose | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Explore | Expose engineering students to entrepreneurial thinking; generate ideas and form teams, with no commitment to continuation. Guest speakers, info sessions, case-competition teams, hackathons. | Faculty and CEED |
| Validate | Convert ideas into validated problems with committed teams, through customer discovery and early prototyping. | CEED, through Product Studio |
| Build | Accelerate validated ventures toward incorporation, capital, and market entry. | CEED, through MakerLaunch |
| Scale | Warm handoff to ecosystem partners with the resources to support growth. | Ecosystem partners, plus the Faculty alumni and PEO network |

The new build at the Validate stage is **Product Studio**. This plan covers its strategic role, resourcing, and targets. Its curriculum, formats, delivery model, and readiness gate are set out in the companion Product Studio document and are not repeated here.

## 3. Key programming changes

Six changes form the core of this revamp.

1. **Establish Product Studio, the Validate-stage program.** This is the missing middle and the central new build. It runs across the year and is anchored by a fall-to-winter Studio Cohort of twelve to sixteen weeks, with non-competitive entry and non-dilutive funding of $500 to $2,500 per team, centred on customer discovery and problem definition.
2. **Co-design an entrepreneurial stream within GNG 4120.** Developed with the course professor over summer 2026, giving teams a venture-continuation off-ramp instead of a course endpoint.
3. **Introduce a capstone entrepreneurial stream by September 2027.** Extends the same off-ramp to capstone teams across the five to six existing engineering concentrations.
4. **Make MakerLaunch entry criteria explicit.** Admission requires evidence of customer discovery or a working prototype, resolving the advisor-feedback issue at its source. The refresh preserves the program's identity and alumni relationships, with one cohort per year until pipeline volume justifies more.
5. **Establish an inbound advisory relationship with the uOttawa Entrepreneurship Hub.** Captures engineering-affiliated founders completing eHub programming into the Faculty pipeline rather than losing them to the broader ecosystem. A no-cost, high-leverage feeder.
6. **Build a deliberate alumni and Professional Engineers of Ontario flywheel.** Operates as the alumni and professional layer of the Scale stage, where the Faculty's comparative advantage is its alumni community and PEO network rather than capital or services.

## 4. Key activities and calendar

The pipeline runs as a connected annual calendar, each activity mapped to a stage. Activity is heaviest at the Explore end, where large events such as the Engineering Case Competitions give students exposure to team formation and competing outside the Faculty, and narrows toward the accelerator. The Simon Nehme Summer Entrepreneurship School is repositioned as the summer entry point to Validate, giving students a full summer to develop ideas before the fall-winter Studio Cohort.

## 5. Proposed resources

The recommended Year 1 ask is the conservative scenario: an incremental operating budget of approximately **$52,600**, in addition to the existing Program Manager salary and space allocation. The program remains single-staffed, with no request for new hires because there is no budget for them. A further non-staff investment in participant capacity and program volume is presented as a staged decision for the September 2027 gate, contingent on demonstrated pipeline volume. The plan is designed to earn any increase in funding by proving the pipeline works first.

| Category | Year 1 (illustrative) | Basis and assumption |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Staff | Ancillary fees | Program Manager salary, already funded; no incremental ask, no new hire |
| Participant capital, Product Studio | $8,000 | 8 teams at about $1,000 non-dilutive validation funding |
| Participant capital, MakerLaunch | $24,000 | About 3 ventures at current per-venture funding; confirm from records |
| Mentor honoraria | $3,000 | Modest; PEO and alumni mentors largely volunteer |
| Events | $7,000 | Summer School, Demo Day, pitch competition, speaker series |
| Space | Existing | Current single allocation; no incremental ask |
| Technology | $3,000 | Lean CRM and cohort-management stack |
| Contracted services | $5,000 | Legal, accounting, and similar; some available in kind |
| Contingency (10%) | ~$2,600 | Applied to new incremental spend |
| **Incremental Year 1 ask** | **~$52,600** | Excludes existing salary and space |

*All figures are illustrative and to be confirmed against CEED and Faculty finance records before submission.* Product Studio does not carry a separate budget; its operating costs sit within the lines above, chiefly Product Studio participant capital, a share of mentor honoraria, and a share of events and technology. A non-staff growth scenario, decided at the September 2027 gate, would expand Validate capacity, the MakerLaunch cohort, and event and service budgets if the pipeline proves out.

### Physical space and visibility

Product Studio currently operates from SITE 3012, which is functional but off-centre, limiting both cohort density and the everyday visibility of entrepreneurship in the Faculty. A visible, high-traffic working space is one of the lowest-cost forms of recruitment: when students see ventures being built, more of them engage. Relocating to, or piloting sessions in, a higher-traffic space such as STEM 034 or the Design Commons would generate passive marketing at the Explore stage and feed Validate and Build over time. This is primarily a space-allocation decision rather than a budget item, which makes it high-leverage. It can begin by hosting sessions in these spaces rather than a full takeover.

## 6. Timeline and phasing

The plan runs over three phases, from September 2026 to September 2029.

### Year 1 (September 2026 to August 2027): Foundation

MakerLaunch is refreshed with explicit entry criteria. Product Studio launches its first fall-winter Studio Cohort. The GNG 4120 entrepreneurial stream is co-designed over summer 2026 and piloted during the academic year. The advisory relationship with the uOttawa Entrepreneurship Hub is established. The MakerLaunch course-credit pathway is activated through existing mechanisms (directed studies, independent project, or co-op designation), so no new course approval is required.

### Year 2 (September 2027 to August 2028): Scale

The capstone entrepreneurial stream launches. The GNG 4120 and capstone streams move from pilot to established. Alumni engagement is formalized. At the September 2027 checkpoint, the Faculty reviews pipeline volume and decides whether to fund the non-staff growth scenario.

### Year 3 (September 2028 to August 2029): Maturity

The alumni and PEO flywheel becomes operational. The 2029 targets for incorporations and qualified applicants come due. The plan is refreshed for the 2029 to 2032 cycle on three years of evidence.

### Quick wins (first 90 days)

- Publish the explicit MakerLaunch entry criteria to resolve the advisor-feedback issue.
- Confirm the no-cost advisory relationship with the uOttawa Entrepreneurship Hub.
- Recruit and confirm the first Studio Cohort.
- Confirm the GNG 4120 summer co-design plan with the course professor.

### Critical path

The single most important dependency for a September 2026 launch is the GNG 4120 summer 2026 co-design, because it rests on the course professor's availability and goodwill rather than on Faculty resources alone. It is sequenced first and flagged accordingly. Product Studio's design proceeds in parallel and is less exposed, since it is a new program under direct CEED control.

## 8. Intended outcomes

By September 2029, the pipeline is designed to triple qualified MakerLaunch applications (from one to eight or more per cycle), roughly double the annual rate of incorporated student-led ventures (from three to five, up to eight to ten a year), and sustain at least twenty alumni returning annually as mentors and contributors.

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| MakerLaunch applications per cycle | 4 | 8 | 20+ |
| MakerLaunch qualified applicants per cycle | 1 | 4 | 8+ |
| Student-led ventures incorporated per year | 3 | 5 | 8 to 10 |
| Studio Cohort size (teams) | n/a | 8 | 16+ |
| GNG 4120 entrepreneurial stream participation | n/a | Pilot cohort | Set after pilot |
| Active alumni returning annually | 3 | 8 | 20+ |
| Founder Net Promoter Score | n/a | Establish baseline | Improve on Year 1 |

This table is the single source of pipeline-level targets. Product Studio's program metrics, in the companion document, feed these outcomes rather than restating them.

## 9. Recommendation

This revamp establishes the validation pipeline MakerLaunch needs to work, at low marginal cost and with no new staff. It is designed to earn any future increase in funding by proving the pipeline works first, with the September 2027 gate as a structured, evidence-based point to consider further non-staff investment once volume is demonstrated.

### Short-term decisions

- Approve the Year 1 scope and the operating budget of approximately **$52,600** above baseline, with no new staff.
- Endorse the September 2026 launch sequence, with the GNG 4120 summer co-design recognized as the critical-path item.
- Pilot a higher-traffic working space, such as STEM 034 or the Design Commons, to make entrepreneurial activity visible across the Faculty.

### Deferred to the September 2027 gate

- Whether to fund the non-staff growth scenario, based on demonstrated pipeline volume.
- Whether pipeline volume justifies a second MakerLaunch cohort, if it can be delivered within single-staff capacity.
